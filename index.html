üß© 7Ô∏è‚É£ Residue Theorem & Integral Formulas
Theorem / Formula Expression
Residue Theorem \( \displaystyle \int_C f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i\sum \text{Res}(f;z_k) \)
Residue (Simple pole) \( \displaystyle \text{Res}(f;z_0)=\lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)f(z) \)
Residue (Pole of order m) \( \displaystyle \text{Res}(f;z_0)=\frac{1}{(m-1)!}\lim_{z\to z_0}\frac{d^{m-1}}{dz^{m-1}}\big[(z-z_0)^mf(z)\big] \)

üß© 8Ô∏è‚É£ Cauchy‚ÄìRiemann Equations (CR)

For \( f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y) \):

\[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \]

Polar form of CR equations:

For \( z = re^{i\theta} \):

\[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial r} = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial v}{\partial \theta}, \quad \frac{\partial v}{\partial r} = -\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u}{\partial \theta} \]

üß© 9Ô∏è‚É£ Harmonic Functions
Concept Formula
A function \(u(x,y)\) is harmonic if \( \nabla^2 u = u_{xx} + u_{yy} = 0 \)
Harmonic conjugate \(v(x,y)\) satisfies \( u_x = v_y, \; u_y = -v_x \)
Together they form \( f(z) = u + iv \) (analytic function)
